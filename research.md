# Corporate Governance Platform

**Project ID:** 435
**Date:** 2026-05-02

## Overview

Corporate governance platforms serve boards of directors, company secretaries, legal teams, and compliance officers by centralising the tools needed to manage meetings, distribute board materials, track entity structures, and maintain compliance with securities regulations including insider trading rules. The market has matured significantly, with AI-powered features now common across leading vendors.

## Problem Statement

Boards of large organisations — particularly public companies and multinationals — must manage sensitive board materials securely, coordinate complex meeting schedules, maintain records of decisions, track entity structures across subsidiaries, and enforce trading blackout windows for insiders. Paper-based or consumer-grade digital tools create information security risks, compliance gaps, and administrative inefficiency. Directors demand intuitive mobile access; regulators demand audit-ready records.

## Core Features

- **Board Portal:** Secure, encrypted distribution of board packs, agendas, minutes, and supporting documents. Mobile-optimised interfaces for directors to annotate materials and access historical records. OnBoard and Diligent are leading examples.
- **Meeting Management:** Agenda construction, pre-read distribution, voting and resolution capture, minute drafting, and action item tracking. AI tools like Govenda's Gabii can generate transcripts and draft minutes automatically.
- **Entity Management:** Registry of all legal entities, subsidiaries, and branches with ownership structures, registered addresses, officer records, and filing deadlines. Diligent supports management of multinational subsidiary networks.
- **Insider Trading Compliance:** Pre-clearance request workflows for director and officer trades, automated trading window management, Section 16 reporting assistance, and audit logs of all clearance decisions.
- **Governance Workflows:** Committee management, delegation of authority matrices, policy approvals, and conflict-of-interest disclosures.
- **Document Management:** Secure repository for governance documents — charters, bylaws, resolutions, contracts — with version control and access permissions.
- **Compliance Tracking:** Monitoring of regulatory filing deadlines, shareholder meeting obligations, and corporate secretarial requirements across jurisdictions.

## Market Landscape

Diligent dominates the enterprise segment with its One Platform approach integrating board management, GRC, and entity management, priced at $15,000–$30,000+ annually for large public companies. OnBoard is rated highly for mid-market usability. Nasdaq Boardvantage serves public-company boards with an emphasis on security. BoardPro and Govenda serve smaller organisations and nonprofits. Board Intelligence focuses on board paper quality and effectiveness. Gartner Peer Insights and Capterra maintain active review categories for this market.

## Key Differentiators

- Security architecture and document encryption at rest and in transit
- AI capabilities for minute drafting, transcript generation, and governance insights
- Depth of entity management across complex multinational structures
- Insider trading workflow completeness and auditability
- Ease of use for non-technical board members

## Technical Considerations

- End-to-end encryption with zero-knowledge key management options
- Integration with legal entity management systems (CT Corporation, Computershare)
- SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certification requirements
- Single sign-on integration with corporate identity providers
- Mobile native apps for iOS and Android with offline access

## Monetisation

Annual SaaS subscription, typically per entity or per board seat. Enterprise contracts for large public companies include dedicated customer success and onboarding. Mid-market and nonprofit tiers at lower price points with self-service setup.
